# TechCorp India — Employee Expense & Compliance Policy
## Version 1 | Effective April 2026
### Inspired by Meta's Concur-based expense framework, adapted for Indian corporate compliance norms

---

> **How to use this document:** Every expense claim submitted by an employee is reviewed
> against the rules below. If a claim violates any rule, it must be Rejected or Escalated
> as specified. If a claim satisfies all applicable rules, it is Approved.
> When in doubt, Escalate — never approve a claim that cannot be clearly validated.

---

## SECTION A — Meals & Food Expenses

**Rule 1 — Small Meal (No Receipt Required)**
Any meal expense **below ₹500** is approved without a receipt.
The employee only needs to provide a brief description (e.g., "lunch with team").
No further documentation is needed.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta's internal Concur policy allows small incidental
> food purchases under a de-minimis threshold without receipt upload,
> reducing auditor workload for low-risk claims.

---

**Rule 2 — Standard Meal (Receipt Required)**
Any meal expense **between ₹500 and ₹2,000 (inclusive)** requires a valid receipt.
The receipt must show: vendor name, date, itemised amounts, and total.
A photo of the bill is acceptable. If the receipt is missing → **Reject**.

> **Real-world basis:** Standard GST compliance in India requires itemised
> receipts for business meals above ₹500 for input tax credit eligibility.

---

**Rule 3 — Large Meal (Receipt + Manager Approval Required)**
Any meal expense **above ₹2,000** requires both a receipt AND a written
manager approval note. If either document is missing → **Reject**.
If the claim has a receipt but no manager note → request the manager note
via RequestInformation before making a final decision.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta enforces a $50 (~₹4,200) per-meal soft limit
> with manager approval required above the threshold, per its 2022 intern
> expense reimbursement guide.

---

**Rule 4 — Alcohol Is Strictly Prohibited**
Alcohol of any kind — wine, beer, spirits — is **never** an approved
business expense, even if consumed during a client dinner or team event.
If any line item on a restaurant bill includes alcohol, the **entire claim
is rejected** — not just the alcohol portion.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta fired an employee earning $400K/year in October
> 2024 for misusing a $25 meal credit on a dinner that included alcohol —
> a direct violation of their no-alcohol expense policy.

## SECTION B — Local Travel

**Rule 5 — Auto-Rickshaw and Metro (No Receipt Required)**
Auto-rickshaw and metro travel **under ₹500** is approved without a receipt.
The employee must provide the origin, destination, and approximate time of travel.

> **Real-world basis:** Indian GST rules exempt unregistered vendors
> (auto drivers) from receipt requirements below the ₹500 threshold.

---

**Rule 6 — Cab Rides After 10:00 PM (Pre-Approved)**
Any cab ride taken **after 10:00 PM** is automatically pre-approved,
provided a valid receipt or ride app screenshot is submitted.
No manager note is required.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta's Hyderabad office provides late-night cab
> reimbursement automatically for employees working past 9 PM.

---

**Rule 7 — Daytime Cab Rides (Manager Note Required)**
Any cab ride taken **before 10:00 PM** requires a manager approval note
explaining the business purpose. If the note is absent → **Reject**.
"Convenience" is not an acceptable reason.

> **Real-world basis:** Ramp's corporate card policy flags daytime ride-shares
> as discretionary travel requiring pre-approval before reimbursement.

---

## SECTION C — Air Travel

**Rule 8 — Economy Class Mandatory (L1–L6)**
All employees at levels **L1 through L6** must fly economy class for
domestic travel. Business class claims from L1–L6 are **automatically rejected**.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta mandates economy class for all non-executive
> domestic flights, with business class restricted to VP-level and above.

---

**Rule 9 — Business Class Permitted (L7 and Above)**
Employees at level **L7 (VP) and above** may book business class for
domestic flights. Self-booked business class requires a VP-signed approval note.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta's E7+ employees are entitled to business class
> domestic travel per the company's travel reimbursement framework.

---

**Rule 10 — International Travel (VP Approval Mandatory)**
Any international travel claim **above ₹50,000** (flights + hotel combined)
requires a **written VP approval email**. If missing → RequestInformation first.
If not provided → **Reject**.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta Finance requires VP sign-off on international
> travel above $600 (~₹50,000), routed through Concur's approval chain.

---

## SECTION D — Remote Work (WFH) Allowances

**Rule 11 — WFH Internet and Electricity Allowance Cap**
WFH internet and electricity claims are capped at **₹1,000 per month**.
Claims above ₹1,000/month are capped — the excess is not reimbursable.
A utility bill is required for any WFH claim above ₹500.

> **Real-world basis:** Indian tech companies including Meta India introduced
> a ₹1,000/month WFH utility allowance post-COVID.

---

## SECTION E — Documentation & Compliance

**Rule 12 — GST Receipt Required Above ₹5,000**
Any single claim **above ₹5,000** must include a GST-compliant receipt
showing the vendor's GSTIN, HSN/SAC code, and itemised tax breakdown.
If the GST receipt is missing → RequestInformation to request it.

> **Real-world basis:** Under Indian GST law (Section 16, CGST Act),
> input tax credit requires a valid tax invoice from a GST-registered vendor.

---

**Rule 13 — Duplicate Claims Are Auto-Rejected**
If the same employee submits two claims with the **same amount on the same date**,
the second claim is automatically rejected as a potential duplicate.

> **Real-world basis:** SAP Concur flags same-day, same-amount duplicates
> as a top-3 expense fraud pattern with auto-rejection as default action.

---

**Rule 14 — L7+ Claims Always Escalate**
Any claim from an employee at **L7 (VP) or above** must be **Escalated**
to the Senior Finance Review team, regardless of amount or category.
Never Approve or Reject L7+ claims directly.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta requires CFO-office review for all VP-and-above
> expenses as part of SOX compliance, preventing self-approval conflicts.

---

**Rule 15 — Personal and Non-Business Expenses Are Rejected**
The following are **never** approved as business expenses:
- Personal shopping (clothing, electronics, gifts for self)
- Gifts without formal gifting approval
- Entertainment without a client present AND without an approval form
- Gym memberships, spa treatments, personal wellness services
- Fines, penalties, or personal legal fees

→ **Reject immediately** for any of the above.

> **Real-world basis:** Meta's "Business Purpose" policy requires all expenses
> to have a clear, documentable business purpose. Personal expenses through
> Concur result in disciplinary action for repeat offenders.
